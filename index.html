Profile

Qiwei Pan is a PhD student in Speech-Language Pathology (Research) at Université de Montréal, based in the COALab at the Centre de recherche Azrieli du CHU Sainte-Justine, under the supervision of Professor Selçuk Güven.

His research uses machine learning for the early identification of developmental speech-language disorders (DSLD) in children. The guiding idea is that DSLD subtypes leave distinguishable acoustic, linguistic, and prosodic traces in children's spontaneous speech. Machine-learning models can pick up these traces for early screening, and explainable-AI methods can turn them into interpretable biomarkers that clinicians are able to validate and trust.

Qiwei reached this field through an interdisciplinary path: first an engineering degree, then a master's in translation and computational linguistics, and now clinical speech AI. The combination of a quantitative engineering base and a deep background in language is the lens he brings to children's speech. Alongside his research, he builds open-source tools that support the speech-processing and annotation workflows his lab relies on. He received a departmental mentorship recognition award in 2026.

Research & Projects

The central project develops AI-assisted early screening of speech-language disorders in multilingual children, organized around three aims.

Aim 1 — Multilingual paediatric clinical dataset

Building on the host laboratory's existing paediatric clinical recordings (French, English, Italian, Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic, and Indigenous languages), expand the corpus with children aged 3–6 across DSLD subtypes, using dual independent speech-language-pathologist annotation (target Cohen's κ ≥ 0.80) and data augmentation.

Aim 2 — Diagnostic model with interpretable outputs

A CNN–Transformer model that takes children's spontaneous speech and produces DSLD subtype classification (target AUROC ≥ 0.85, sensitivity/specificity ≥ 0.80), with cognitive-score prediction as an auxiliary task. A disorder-specific speech recognition model is fine-tuned on the same data as an enabling tool for clinical transcription.

Aim 3 — Cross-linguistic biomarkers via explainable AI

Using attention visualization, LIME, and SHAP, decode the model's decisions, identify the acoustic, phonological, and prosodic features that drive DSLD predictions across languages, and validate them as candidate clinical biomarkers through structured speech-language-pathologist ratings.
